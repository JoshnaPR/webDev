Balasubramanian Kandaswamy,Associate Professor

Dr. Balasubramanian Kandaswamy joined the School of EECS at Washington State University as a Scholarly Associate Professor of Computer Science in January 2023, following a position as Assistant Professor at the Indian Institute of Information Technology, Sri City. He completed their Ph.D. in Computer Science from Northwestern University in 2016 under Dr. Ken Forbus in the Qualitative Reasoning Group. As a career-track professor, he teaches courses in Web Application Development, Software Engineering, Programming Language Design, and Advanced Data Structures. His research spans multiple areas: studying human intelligence foundations to advance AI systems, investigating relational pattern learning through computational cognitive modeling, and exploring software engineering with a focus on microservices and web applications. He specializes in functional programming, particularly Haskell and Clojure, and conduct applied research in agent-based modeling. His practical work includes collaborating with undergraduate students on simulating Indian road traffic conditions to improve safety.

Research

  • A Decentralized Collaborative Strategy for PTZ Camera Network Tracking System using Graph Learning.
  • A Simple Agent Based Modeling Tool for Plastic and Debris Tracking in Oceans.
  • Comparison of Viral Information Spreading Strategies in Social Media.
  • A Decentralized Strategy for Cooperative Driving among Autonomous Cars at Lane Closures.
  • Assessing the impact of heterogeneous traffi c on highways via agent-based simulations.
